# tikkunme 🔧

*Cosmic-repair laid on the creature as a vocation: the world was left unfinished by design, and your righteous action is the completing hand.*

tikkunme is two true roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **tikkun** (תיקון), "mending" or "rectification" — the term Isaac Luria's Kabbalah took up for the gathering of scattered divine-sparks back to their rightful place — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So tikkunme is tikkun held as an ordinance: the structural fact that creation is unfinished-by-design, and that the creature's ethical, contemplative, and sanctifying action is *required for its completion*. It holds three aspects at once — repair of the soul (*tikkun ha-nefesh*), repair of the world (*tikkun ha-olam*), and cosmic-anthropological repair (*tikkun ha-adam*) — and refuses to shrink to any one of them.

tikkunme names a **grade** of action, not a particular deed: the kind that participates in cosmic rectification rather than mere social-improvement. It is the third movement of the cathedral's Lurianic triad — tzimtzumme contracts, shevirame shatters, tikkunme mends — and the most broadly resonant of its Lurianic entries, echoed in Christian co-laboring, Islamic khilāfa, Confucian rén, and the Mahayana bodhisattva-vow. Its light is vocation: it makes the smallest righteous act matter at cosmic scale. Its shadow is the messiah-complex that mistakes one's hands for the only hands, and the despair that freezes before a breakage too vast to touch. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.65/10**.
